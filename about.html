About Me

I am a geomatics and remote sensing professional with a multidisciplinary background spanning geospatial analysis, environmental monitoring, logistics coordination, and hospitality management. I studied Geographical Engineering in Peru, which marked the beginning of my career in geomatics, where I developed strong skills in spatial data processing, photo-interpretation, and environmental analysis. I later completed advanced studies in Marine Sciences in Spain, earning the Diploma de Estudios Avanzados (DEA) in Environmental Technology. My research focused on predicting bathing water quality using geospatial analysis and numerical modeling, strengthening my analytical thinking and my interest in environmental monitoring systems.

Alongside my technical path, I gained professional experience in logistics management and hospitality in Spain and United States. These roles strengthened my leadership, communication, adaptability, and operational coordination skills in fast-paced environments.

After returning to geomatics, I pursued a Master’s degree in Geomatics and Remote Sensing, where my research focused on applying computer vision to agriculture. I developed a YOLO-based model for phenological stage detection in vineyards, reinforcing my expertise in remote sensing and geospatial artificial intelligence.

I have competencies in GIS (QGIS, ArcGIS), remote sensing workflows, Python used in academic geospatial tasks (AI‑supported), spatial analysis, operational coordination, and team collaboration. I am particularly interested in precision agriculture, environmental monitoring, and other applied geospatial domains.

My objective is to integrate the geomatics and remote sensing sector in Québec. I am open to junior roles, internships, or collaborative projects where I can contribute my technical skills while continuing to grow in advanced geospatial technologies, including AI and computer vision.
